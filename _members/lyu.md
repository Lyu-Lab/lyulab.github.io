description: "Jiankun (JK) Lyu is an Assistant Professor and head of the Evnin Family Laboratory of Computational Molecular Discovery at The Rockefeller University.

He earned his B.E. (2013) and Ph.D. (2019) at the East China University of Science & Technology, then completed his postdoctoral training in [Brian Shoichet's lab](https://shoichetlab.compbio.ucsf.edu/) at UCSF in 2022, where he developed ultra-large library docking approaches that screen hundreds of millions to billions of make-on-demand molecules against biological targets.

His lab studies the molecular recognition between small molecules and their targets, develops new molecular docking algorithms, applies them to understudied targets with therapeutic potential, and builds computational tools for new chemogenetic systems. JK is a faculty member in the David Rockefeller Graduate Program, the Tri-Institutional Ph.D. Program in Chemical Biology, and the Tri-Institutional Ph.D. Program in Computational Biology & Medicine. He received the Irma T. Hirschl and Monique Weill-Caulier Trust Research Award (2023) and was named a Searle Scholar (2024)."
